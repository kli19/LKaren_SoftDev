GRID & ALERTS: Jellyfish Myths Busted

Jellyfish are all the same species
MYTH

FACT: There are more than 200 documented species of true jellyfish (and many more of their stinging relatives) across the globe.
Jellyfish "go after" people
MYTH

FACT: Any contact with jellyfish is incidental. Humans are not on their menu, but when we are in their environment we can get in the way of their tentacles. While jellyfish don't have a brain, they can sense light and have coordinated swimming behaviors, which help keep them in good places to hunt for microscopic plants and fish eggs/larvae, or other prey like fish, worms, and crustaceans.
Applying urine to a jellyfish sting can reduce the pain
MYTH

FACT: Perhaps the most interesting of myths, the use of urine to treat stings has been tested and proven unhelpful. A better idea? Try an acidic liquid like vinegar. There are also several commercially available products marketed for stings.
What to do if you get stung: First, look for any tentacle adhering to skin, and flush the area well with cold ocean water. Do not rub the sting area because you could inadvertently distribute the venom further into the body. Then vinegar or evidence-based commercial product should be applied if there is continuing pain.

GRID SYSTEM

Bootstrap uses a fully responsive grid system of containers, rows, and columns to format content.
Some rules to follow when using the grid system:
  • Place rows inside containers
  • Only columns may be the immediate children of rows
  • The grid system allows you to divide a row into 12 columns of equal width. You can combine these columns, but the number of columns the containers in a single row span across cannot exceed 12.
Resize the browser window to see the effect on the grid above!

Here is sample grid code!


	    <div class="container-fluid">
	    <div class="row">
	    <div class="col-md-2" style="background-color:lavender;">.col-md-2</div>
	    <div class="col-md-4" style="background-color:lavenderblush;">.col-md-4</div>
	    <div class="col-md-6" style="background-color:lavender;">.col-md-6</div>
	    </div>
	    </div>

ALERTS

Alerts provide feedback for user actions. Take a look at this code: 

	    <div class="container">  
	    <div class="alert alert-success">  
	    <strong>Success!</strong> Well done its submitted.  
	    </div>  
	    <div class="alert alert-info">  
	    <strong>Info!</strong> take this info.  
	    </div>  
	    <div class="alert alert-warning">  
	    <strong>Warning!</strong> Dont submit this.  
	    </div>  
	    <div class="alert alert-danger">  
	    <strong>Danger!</strong> Change few things.  
	    </div>  
	    </div>

This code results in the following alerts (though there are also the primary, secondary, light, and dark alerts):

Success! Well done its submitted.
Info! take this info.
Warning! Dont submit this.
Danger! Change few things.
